    I am a third-year Computer Engineering student at Queen's University, specializing in Software Engineering. I’m passionate about creating impactful software solutions and solving complex problems. My technical expertise spans programming languages like Java, Python, and C++, as well as frameworks such as React.js and Spring Boot.

    Currently, I am working on designing a CPU based on a RISC-style architecture, gaining hands-on experience in digital systems engineering and low-level hardware design. This complements my previous experience, such as developing a full-stack Fantasy Basketball website that displays player statistics, built using Spring Boot and PostgreSQL and deployed with Firebase and Heroku. I also co-founded the Queen’s Cybersecurity and Cryptography Club, where I lead projects focused on secure software development.

    I am actively seeking software development internships where I can work across the stack and contribute to building robust APIs. Additionally, I am open to hardware development internships that involve low-level computer engineering and system design. My diverse experience in software and hardware gives me a unique perspective to bridge the gap between both domains.
